Pathways: College and Career Exploration

As high school and undergraduate students navigate the complex landscape of college and career choices, they often encounter overwhelming amounts of digital information, leading to difficulty in making informed decisions. To address this problem, this thesis project developed Pathways, an interactive college and career exploration website designed to engage and assist students in their search process. The website provides a space-themed, interactive interface to help users explore different career paths, discover potential majors and courses of study, and identify schools and programs that align with their interests and goals.

Methods

  1. Literature Review | Explored various publications regarding interactive visualization systems, educational technologies, and career decision self-efficacy.
  2. Competitive Analysis | Conducted market research on other existing technology that addresses college and career exploration.
  3. Prototyping | Created low and high fidelity prototypes of system interface and conducted several design iterations before chosing the final interface.
  4. Stakeholder Research | While in development, presented system to individuals in the field of education to obtain feedback and implement changes in parallel for user-informed design.
  5. User Research | Led user studies with current undergraduate students, testing the usability of the Pathways website and creating a comparison to an existing competitor, College Board.
